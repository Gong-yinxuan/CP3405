\## 4. Key Cross-Asset Story Breakdown

The trading week experienced a complete, aggressive mirror reversal of the previous week's risk-off panic. Market volatility collapsed completely, with the VIX (−6.25%) tying for the week's biggest laggard as option market panic totally dissipated, pushing the fear gauge down to 17.90. Investors aggressively returned to risk assets, completely rotating out of defensive energy positions as WTI Crude ($84.29) and Brent Crude ($86.80) both plunged over 6%.

Instead of a narrow mega-cap technology squeeze, the market breadth widened significantly. Mega-cap tech and communication networks sat out the rally, with Technology (−0.30%) and Communications (−1.90%) lagging the broader index as capital rotated away from heavy clusters. Traditional bond proxies also struggled to catch a bid early on, leaving Utilities (−0.10%) stranded in negative territory for the week despite a late-stage relief bounce.

In stark contrast, broad cyclical, financial, and stable asset pockets outperformed. Materials (+2.40%) experienced a massive structural bid to significantly outpace the market. Financials (+2.30%) proved to be a dominant engine, building an early lead above the benchmark and preserving its strength through Friday's close. Real Estate (+1.40%) shook off a deep early-week liquidation to post an impressive trend decoupling alongside Industrials (+1.30%). Consumer Staples (+1.70%) and Healthcare (+1.10%) demonstrated strong stability, decoupling cleanly through the mid-week equity dip to easily beat the index. Consumer Discretionary (+0.90%) notched positive returns despite turbulent intra-week downswings, while Energy (+0.10%) barely scratched out a positive finish after being heavily anchored by the crude liquidation.

Domestic small caps ultimately took the crown with the Russell 2000 surging (+3.93%) to close at 2,949.20, vastly outperforming the flatter S\&P 500 (+0.46%). This global appetite for risk was amplified by a massive run in international equities like the Nikkei 225 (+4.81%) and an aggressive capital return to crypto, pushing Bitcoin (+5.20%) up to $63,490.00 to top the macro leaderboard.
