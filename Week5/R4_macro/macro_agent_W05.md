## MACRO BIAS
**Slightly Bearish**

Markets are under pressure from geopolitical uncertainty around the Strait of Hormuz and upcoming US–Iran talks. While lower yields provide some support, inflation concerns are keeping bond yields elevated and limiting upside for risk assets.

Recent macro signals suggest slower growth combined with sticky inflation, which creates a cautious “stagflation-like” environment and reduces risk-on conviction.

## CONFIDENCE
**Medium**

The view is supported by inflation concerns, cautious central bank expectations, and geopolitical risk. However, confidence is limited because outcomes from upcoming negotiations could quickly improve sentiment.

## INVALIDATION
This view is invalidated if geopolitical tensions ease significantly, the Fed turns more dovish, inflation expectations fall below 4%, or the US dollar weakens back below the 100 level.
